Albert Einstein

German physicist

Timeline Of Albert Einstein

  • 1879 - Albert Einstein, German-born physicist, Nobel Prize laureate (d. 1955)
  • 1900 - Max Planck discovers the law of black-body radiation (Planck's law), by introducing the notion of light quanta, leading in 1905 to Albert Einstein's explanation of the photoelectric effect and beginning the Quantum Revolution.
  • 1905 - 1905 Albert Einstein submits for publication his paper "On a heuristic viewpoint concerning the production and transformation of light", in which he explains the photoelectric effect using the notion of light quanta (published June 9).
  • 1915 - Albert Einstein presents part of his theory of general relativity to the Prussian Academy of Sciences.
  • 1931 - Albert Einstein begins doing research at the California Institute of Technology, along with astronomer Edwin Hubble.
